Grounding in reality. Your actual story. The pain you've seen and solved.

## The Real Story

### Your Background
- 30 years in software (since the web began, 1995)
- Key engineer at startup 1995, wore all hats for 5 years
- Left corporate 13 years ago to consult for startups
- 13 years refining the ideal SaaS foundation
- CTO of 3 startups
- Seen countless projects grow and suffer from early decisions

### What You've Witnessed
- Projects start from templates and frameworks (you take the good with the bad)
- Uncomfortable parts get worse as it grows
- Things need redoing when scaling features or users
- Iterative pain: technology selection, team decisions, agile practices
- Early decisions haunt projects for years

### What The Codebase Is
- 13 years of refinement (not 30)
- Looks at future pain and prevents it for the next project
- Curated open-source libraries with specific patterns to avoid pain
- The lessons learned, baked into code

## The Pain (More Real Now)

### The Template Trap
> "Every project starts from templates and frameworks. You take the good with the bad.
>
> The bad gets worse as you grow. Features don't scale. Users break things. Early decisions haunt you.
>
> Eventually, you're redoing what you already built."

### The Iterative Pain
> "I've watched it for 30 years. Project starts fast. Then the friction comes.
>
> Scaling features. Scaling users. Technology that seemed fine at 100 users collapses at 10,000.
>
> Most of it was preventable. If someone had known what was coming."

### The Early Decision Problem
> "The decisions you make in week one define year three.
>
> Technology selection. Architecture. Team structure. Agile practices.
>
> Get them wrong and you're rebuilding. Get them right and everything compounds."

>

>
> When you start here, you skip the pain I've seen a hundred times."
