## Problem Angles to Explore

### Angle 1: The Complexity Wall
You're building fast with AI. It feels amazing. Then suddenly nothing works, everything breaks, and you realize you built a house of cards.

**Problem options:**
- "Vibe coding is fast—until you hit the wall."
- "AI lets you build 10x faster. It also lets you fail 10x faster."
- "You shipped in a week. Now nothing scales and everything breaks."
- "The faster you vibe code, the harder you crash."

### Angle 2: Toys vs. Real Software
There's a difference between a demo and a product. Between something that works on your laptop and something that works with 10,000 users.

**Problem options:**
- "There's vibe coding. And there's building real software."
- "Your prototype works. Will it survive customers?"
- "Demos don't need architecture. Products do."
- "Built on hallucinations and blog code?"

### Angle 3: The Foundation Gap
Everyone starts from scratch. Everyone reinvents the wheel. Meanwhile, experienced teams ship on proven foundations.

**Problem options:**
- "Starting from scratch is starting behind."
- "Every hour on scaffolding is an hour not on product."
- "Your competitors with experienced teams aren't reinventing auth."
- "You're building on nothing. They're building on years of patterns."

### Angle 4: Alone in the Wilderness
You're a founder with an idea. Maybe you can code, maybe you can't. Either way, you're trying to figure out what "production-ready" even means.

**Problem options:**
- "Building software alone is building in the dark."
- "Who reviews your architecture when it's just you?"
- "You don't know what you don't know about scaling."
- "The first 10 technical decisions will haunt you for years."

## "This is Me" Test

The right founder reads this and thinks: "Finally. Someone who gets it."

**Who is that founder?**
- Has vibe-coded something, knows it's fragile
- Smart enough to know they don't know everything
- Funded (or about to be) and needs to ship fast
- Burned by agencies or offshore teams before
- Wants senior attention, not junior execution
- Sees the value of joining something, not just hiring someone

**Disqualifying signals (not "this is me"):**
- "I just need cheap developers"
- "I built it myself and it's perfect, just need help deploying"
- "I don't have any money but my idea is amazing"
- "I want to control every technical decision"
